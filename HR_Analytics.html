# Filter only employees who left
attrition_data = hr[hr['Attrition'] == 'Yes']

# Count attrition per department
dept_counts = attrition_data['Department'].value_counts()

# Plot pie chart
plt.figure(figsize=(6,4))
plt.pie(dept_counts, labels=dept_counts.index, autopct='%1.1f%%', startangle=90)
plt.title("Department-wise Attrition")
plt.show()

Department Wise Attrition

Research & Development 56.1%

This department contributes the highest attrition.

More than half of total employee exits are from R&D.

Insight: Possible reasons could be workload pressure, skill-market demand, role stress, or limited growth opportunities.

Business Risk: High impact area losing technical talent affects productivity & innovation.

Sales 38.8%

Second highest attrition contributor.

Almost 2 out of every 5 employees leaving are from Sales.

Insight: Could indicate performance pressure, targets, incentives, or job stress.

Business Risk: Revenue and customer relationships may be impacted.
